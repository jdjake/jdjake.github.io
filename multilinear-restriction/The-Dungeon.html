The Dungeon

The Dungeon

Mar 04, 202613 min read

A huge tower sitting atop a mountain, with a league-wide base. From the foot of the mountain, it is impossible to see the top. There are many legends about the place. But they all have one commonality; an adventurer can make their fortune there, if they are willing to risk their life.

But why is The Dungeon filled with such disparate bands of monsters. Who resets the traps? Why, after being plundered so many times, is The Dungeon still filled with such riches.

The answer is an extremely well-kept secret. Within The Dungeon, there is a company, whose mission is to lure adventurers into the dungeon, eliminate them, and rob them. You can think of the operation like a casino - some adventurers escape with a meager supply of loot, but most do not, and so The House Always Wins. The wealth contributed by the adventurers who lose their lives there allows the organization to survive and (in good years) even to prosper.

Your characters are employees of Dungeon & Co. They are even the essential cog in the organization: receptionists. Their job is to welcome customers, i.e. adventurers, and to kill them and steal their belongings. Always remember: A Good Customer is a Dead Customer.

Below is a description of several key members of the customer, and a description of the heirarchy.

The Chief Administrator

The Chief administrator is a man called Mr. Noir. He is the founder of the company and sole owner of the corporation. Not much is known about him, and few claim to have ever met him (and those that have are all bigwigs). Mr. Noir, apart from his inner circle (personal assistants, secretaries, etc) has only two direct subordinates, each heads of one of the divisions of the company: The Strategy Division and the Operation Division. Since little is known about Mr. Noir, this fuels rumors: is he a demon hatching a long-term plan to corrupt humanity? Or perhaps a wealthy heir who needed something to do with his family fortune? Or is he a vampire who haunts the corridors after hours to feed at night? In short, since no one knows anything, anything is possible…

The Strategy Division

The Strategy Division is in charge of the long-term vision of the organization. This division includes the Treasury Department, Living Resources Department, and Marketing Department.

The Treasury Department

The Treasury Department manages everything related to the finances of Dungeon Inc., whether income and expenses (Accounting) or investments (Patron Relations).

Accounting

Accounting draws up the annual budget for Dungeon Inc. and determines resources allocated to each department, as well as securing the revenue of the company. This involves not only collecting all the booty collected by the Receptionists, but also reselling items of value. Accounting prefers to hire employees who are good with numbers, and unpretentious as possible - rigor and discipline are virtues. Therefore, there are a high proportion of creatures with superior intelligence (vampires, mind-flayers, etc) but also structured and rigid-minded creatures (Constructs, Golems, Modrons, etc).

Patron Relations

Responsible for drawing in sponsors likely to invest in the development of Dungeon Inc. as well as maintaining good relations with them (through events, cocktail parties, symposiums, etc). Also negotiates via accounting the renumeration of sponsors during annual reviews. Patron Relations primarily employs monsters who have a network of contacts in the upper echelons of society, or who are charismatic and sticklers for etiquette. Employees generally impress with their elegant appearance and ability to charm (naturally or magically). Therefore there are a higher proportion of elves, devils, incubi, succubi, lamias, etc.

Department of Live Resources

The Living Resources department is responsible for recruiting employees, paying salaries, and managing contracts, but also for relations with inhabitants of the dungeon.

Recruitment Department

The Recruitment department is responsible for identifying the skills required for the various missions of Dungeon Inc. and recruiting the right candidates. Candidates are drawn from creatures who frequent the dungeon, or in very rare cases, from Clients. However, sometimes the Recruitment Department may look outside the Dungeon for candidates with specific profiles. Recruitment generally hires employees who are sensitive to the needs of others, but also skilled negotiators. In addition, the team insists on diversity among its members in order to maximize the chances of establishing a rapport with a candidate regardless of their background.

Internal Relations Department

The role of internal relations stems from a single observation: why send costly resources to eliminate Clients when you can put adversaries in their path who will do the job without getting paid? The role of internal relations is therefore to attract monsters to suitable levels of The Dungeon and ensure they stay in their place, and eliminate the adventurers (or at least weaken them before the arrival of a team of Receptionists). Internal relations are undeniably the team that enjoys the greatest diversity in terms of profiles, particularly in terms of races represented. Indeed, it is necessary to know how to play on the affinities of races with little developed intelligence to convince them to settle (or stay) in an area of the Dungeon despite the potential dangers. Its employees are often people who have traveled extensively and know the world well, but are also smooth talkers and unscrupulous.

Termination Department

Keeping the existence of Dungeon Inc. a secret is of vital importance to the corporation’s success. Discretion is key, and that is where Termination comes in. officially, Termination manages the termination of contracts with employees. Unofficially, they are in charge of eliminating anyone who might be likely to spill the beans, and former employees are at the top of the list. They are also responsible for terminating the contracts of indiscreet employees. The selection criteria for joining the Termination team are quite simple: you must be an excellent killer; you must be discreet; you must be loyal. Beyond that, Termination has no use for any other qualities or flaws, so you’ll find all kinds of brutes who know how to control themselves, and silent assassins.

Marketing Department

The central role of Marketing is to attract customers to the Dungeon. To do this, it is necessary to design attractive areas and carry out appropriate promotional campaigns. To be effective, it is therefore necessary to know the customers well.

Design Department

in order to maintain a constant flow of adventurers, you need to attract them by offering them something new. Although the Dungeon is a place with an unpredictable geography, it remains a unique location on a vast map that has no shortage of other points of interest for adventurers. Whether they are greedy, reckless, or righteous, they must be confronted with situations (concepts, in Design jargon) that are new and intriguing. The Design team therefore invents areas, with appropriate histories and recommendations for Advertising. The Packaging team is then responsible for manufacturing them to the required specifications. The Design Department are a mix of creatives (whose role is to come up with concepts) and architects (whose role is to turn their colleagues’ ideas into plans and, if necessary, temper them with a touch of pragmatism). The team is rounded out by managers, whose role is to evaluate the costs and expected revenues of each concept. The Design department is therefore made up of intelligent people who are generally free-spirited (at least more so than average).

Publicity Department

All of the Publicity Department’s intellectual and financial resources are devoted to getting to know customers (this is profiling) and classifying them as greedy (attracted by gold), reckless (attracted by the thrill of risk) and vigilant (attracted by the possibility of putting an end to evil) (attracted by the thrill of risk) and avengers (attracted by the possibility of putting an end to evil) and luring them into the areas most likely to cause their demise. Advertising uses many methods: spreading rumors in inns, scattering treasure maps, raids, and kidnappings. In short, anything that will motivate adventurers to come and try their luck in the Dungeon. The Department obviously seeks to attract creative profiles, but also people with a somewhat mercenary mentality, who are likely to think cynically about what is most likely to appeal to a particular type of adventurer. In addition to creative teams, La Réclame employs artists (to design the accessories needed for advertising campaigns) and field teams, who work primarily outdoors.

Operations Division

The Operations Division is responsible for ensuring the day-to-day running of Donjon & Co.: maintenance, supervision, expansion, and, of course, customer service.

Facilities Department

The Facilities Department undoubtedly has the widest variety of tasks in the organization: feeding employees, but also resetting traps, ensuring areas comply with regulations, and mapping them.

Department of Maintainance

Maintenance is the backbone of the Dungeon, ensuring both the core mission by making sure that areas open to customers are ready, and that the rest of the staff can work in good conditions. Most employees believe that Maintenance spends its time resetting traps and repairing damage done by customers (or vandals) to the areas. But Maintenance is also responsible for the living and working quarters of Donjon & Co., the various canteens, and a thousand other services that employees are happy to enjoy on a daily basis. Maintenance hires all kinds of profiles, but is primarily looking for candidates who are hard workers. Maintenance activities are, for the most part, very physical, and the department therefore favors strong, willing employees with recognized mechanical skills. There are more dwarves and gnomes here than in any other department.

Supplies Department

The Supplies Department has a dual role. On the one hand, the department manages all the equipment needs of the company’s various activities: the packages for the hosts (obviously), but also the tools for Maintenance, the supplies for Surveying, the crystal balls for Supervision, etc. On the other hand, Equipment manages the collection of customers’ equipment and valuables. They must therefore identify what is valuable (and which is transferred to Entries and Exits) and what can be reused for Donjon & Cie missions. There are very different jobs within Equipment and therefore, inevitably, very varied profiles. The stewards who manage equipment needs are a combination of office clerks (endowments, inventories, storage, etc.), craftsmen (damaged equipment must be repaired, anything that can be useful for the company’s missions must be transformed, etc.) and managers. In general, In general, Equipment favors manual and logistical profiles.

Surveying Department

Surveying is in charge of both regularly visiting the various areas of the dungeon (those open to customers and others) and maintaining accurate maps of them. These maps are not only used by Receptionists when they are on assignment, but also by Marketing (to avoid having to reinvent the wheel with each new area), Maintenance (to know where work needs to be done), Supervision (to locate Customers), and so on. In short, it is a vital function of Dungeon Inc. and yet probably the most employees complain about. To work in surveying requires observation, love of walking, and a precise hand and attention to detail. Surveying relies on versatile individuals who go into the field and draw or update maps. It employs many gnomes, who possess both nimble hands and a taste for exploration. It also boasts the highest concentration of glasses and monocles: Surveying is not kind to the eyesight of its employees.

Reception Department

Reception is the backbone of Donjon & Co.’s business model, as its members are responsible for eliminating customers and robbing them. It is (by far) the largest team within the company, but also the one with the highest mortality rate.

Supervision Department

The core mission of Supervision is to know times where The Dungeon’s guests are so that Receptionists can be directed when necessary. It’s possible (but these are undoubtably malicious rumors) that these methods are used to spy on Dungeon Inc. employees. Supervision employees use a combination of mechanical means (primarily alarms) and magical ones (crystal balls, remote viewing spells, etc). Therefore, more than in any other department of the organization, magical creatures and other spellcasters have a place in Supervision. An affinity for magic is the main criterion, although having more eyes than normal or other heightened senses is also valued.

Reception Department

Reception is the backbone of Donjon & Co.’s business model, as its members are responsible for eliminating customers and robbing them. It is (by far) the largest team within the company, but also the one with the highest mortality rate. Reception is not very discerning about the nature of its employees, given that their lifespan is limited. That being said, an aptitude for violence or an ability to use offensive magic are obviously prized. So you’ll find all kinds of creatures who are used to facing adventurers, from minotaurs to vampires, ogres, trolls, and mummies. The only restrictions on employment within Reception are the ability to follow orders and the physical ability to move around the corridors of the Dungeon.

Expansion Department

The Dungeon is not a static place and, despite its immense size, must constantly expand to open up new areas and attract more and more customers. It is the role of Expansion Management to ensure this

Exploration Department

In order to attract more and more new customers, we must constantly offer them new areas to explore. The good news is that the depths of the Dungeon are virtually infinite. The bad news is that most of these places are dangerous and little known. The role of Exploration is to prospect and secure them so that we can build new customer areas. The role of Exploration is to prospect and secure them so that we can build new customer areas there. The qualities required to work in Exploration are both physical (you have to be hard-working, willing to take risks, in short, be in exceptional physical shape) and mental (you must be fearless and have an iron will). Certain species that are particularly well-suited to underground life are favored, whether they be troglodytes, who are flexible and comfortable in underground tunnels and underwater, or deep dwarves, who are experts in rock and comfortable in the abyss.

Reconditioning Department

The role of Packaging is to build customer areas in new parts of the Dungeon secured by Exploration. The concepts are produced by Design (within Marketing), but it is Reconditioning that manages their manufacture. This often involves compromises between the two entities to limit costs (but also because some concepts from Marketing are… a bit crazy?). Most employees in Packaging are manual laborers or site managers. Their profiles are not very different from those in Maintenance: they are strong, hard-working, mechanically skilled, and resourceful. While the job is difficult, it is also relatively low-risk, so Packaging employees are often loyal to their department and stay there for a long time. This is the department with the most old timers.
