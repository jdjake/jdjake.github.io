Character Creation

Creating a Character:

  1. Print Out a Character Sheet, or draw your own on a sheet of paper. Your Character Sheet needs space for a Character Name, Attributes, Hit Protection, Currency, a Failed Career, and 10 Inventory Slots. Leave space to add other details about your Character and their relationships to others in the crew.
  2. Roll 3d6 for each of the three Attributes, STRength, DEXterity, WILLpower, in order. After you have rolled, you may then pick two of the results and swap them, if you’d like.
  3. Characters start with d6 Hit Protection, representing their ability to avoid taking serious damage.
  4. Characters start with d6 × 2 New Pounds (£), enough to buy a couple sandwiches of varying quality.
  5. You had a shot at a normal life and you blew it. Match your highest Attribute with your lowest to determine your Failed Career. Your Hit Protection and Starting Currency determine further features related to your failed career.
  6. Choose a Name for your character. Here’s some inspirational tips:
    • Some folks have one name, others have multiple.
    • Some have nicknames, aliases, family names, or village names.
    • Some names come with descripters, sometimes accurate, and sometimes false (Bujamew the Magnanimous, Fragrant Joe, etc).
    • Some names describe a character’s class (Agu the Money Lender, Bilbo the Burglar, etc).
    • Take inspiration from the names associated with your Failed Career, or more general Tables of names.
  7. Choose some Character Traits to distinguish your character, or Roll to give you inspiration.
  8. Randomly choose three other characters from the crew, and form Connections with them. Maybe you knew each other before you became part of the crew, or maybe your Connection represents your initial hunches about them?

Further Character Development

When a session concludes, answer one of the following questions:

  • Why did your character become imprisoned. What prompted them to subject themselves to the bondage contract of the Martel Trading Company?

  • What does your character value the most, and what has it cost you so far to maintain that value? Use the answer to this question to come up with a Core Belief your character might hold. For inspiration, you may want to consider other Characters Core Beliefs, and either choose ones that go with or go against those beliefs.

  • Think of a time your adventurer struggled with something while they were young, that they are still ashamed of today.

  • What was the moment that you realized your Failed Career wasn’t, or couldn’t be, right for you?

  • Back in The City, there’s someone who’d be disappointed to see you now. Who are they, and why?

  • There was a rumor about this island you dismissed back in prison. But now it seems true? What was the rumor, and what does it say about you that you dismissed it?

During a short rest, one of the party may choose to tell others their thoughts about the questions above (telling them their story, admitting their shames, etc). They then gain a Bond that lasts until the next short rest. The Character can spend this bond to gain advantage on a roll of their choosing.

Once you’ve checked off at least three of the questions above, and you’ve formed at least six Connections with other characters, add d4 to your Hit Protection.

Forming Connections

When a session concludes:

You may form a new Connection with a character from the session you’ve just played. This connection may be related to events between the characters in the present, or a past before our campaign (feel free to invent past events). Describe your connection, perhaps in reference from one of several Archetypes.

Connections are not fixed in stone. Review your current Connections, and discuss with other players if you feel your interactions in game have strengthened, weakened, or fundamentally adjusted the relationship you both have with one another.

When you admit your shame to another player during a short rest, you may add one to an attribute relevant to the story.

In a future session, if you take a short rest to tell others from the crew about this story, you may subtract one point from a relevant attribute, and add one point to an attribute relevant to the responses of the crew to your story.
