Character Creation

Creating a Character:

  1. Roll 3d6 for each of the three attributes, STRength, DEXterity, WILLpower, in order. You may then swap any two of the results.
  2. Characters start with d6 Hit Protection, representing their ability to avoid taking serious damage.
  3. Characters start with 2d6 New Pounds (£), enough to buy a couple sandwiches, or varying quality.
  4. You had a shot at a normal life and you blew it. Match your highest Attribute with your lowest to determine your Failed Career. Your Hit Protection and Starting Currency determine further features related to your failed career.
  5. Choose a Name for your character. Some folks have one name, others have multiple. Some have nicknames, aliases, family names, or village names. Some names come with descripters, sometimes accurate, and sometimes false (Bujamew the Magnanimous, Fragrant Joe, etc). Some names describe a character’s class (Agu the Money Lender, Bilbo the Burglar, etc). Take inspiration from the names associated with your Failed Career, or more general Tables of names.
  6. Choose some Character Traits to distinguish your character, or Roll to give you inspiration.

Further Character Development

When a session concludes, answer one of the following questions:

  • Why did your character become imprisoned. What prompted them to subject themselves to the bondage contract of the Martel Trading Company? In a future session, if you take a short rest to tell others from the crew about this story, you may add one to a relevant attribute.

  • What does your character value the most, and what has it cost you so far to maintain that value? Use the answer to this question to come up with a Core Belief your character might hold. Bonus points if this value is something that could come into conflict with the values of the other characters in your crew.

  • Think of a time your adventurer struggled with something while they were young, that they are still ashamed of today. When you admit your shame to another player during a short rest, you may add one to an attribute relevant to the story.

In addition, you may also form a new Connection with a character from the session you’ve just played. This connection may be related to events between the characters in the present, or a past before our campaign (feel free to invent past events, provided that the other character agrees). Describe your connection, perhaps in reference from one of several Archetypes.

Connections are not fixed in stone. In subsequent sessions, Connections between characters may change. At the end of each session, discuss with other players if you feel your interactions in game have strengthened, weakened, or changed completely the relationship with one another.

Once each question has been answered, and you’ve formed at least three connections with other characters, add d4 to your Hit Protection.
