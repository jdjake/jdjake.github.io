Wilderness Travel

In this campaign, you’ll be navigating Hot Springs Island, which is made up of twenty-five Hexes, each 2 miles in diameter. Each hex contains 3 points of interest to discover and explore. These points are all physical locations that can be revisited, rather than one-time events or encounters. The Hot Springs Field Guide contains a log to keep track of which locations you’ve visited.

Time

In order to abstract time to the level of wilderness travel, we divide a day into six 4-hour units of time, called Watches. Days are divided up into six 4-hour Watches (Dawn, Day, Day, Dusk, Night, Night). At Dawn on each day, the Weather is rolled. Then the Party decides how they want to spend their day. Each of the following Actions takes 1 Watch: Navigation, Investigation, and Regrouping,

Weather

At the start of each day, the GM rolls for Weather. They roll a d6:

  • On a 1-3, the weather is Favorable - clear skies and a slight breeze.
  • On a 4-5, the weather is Unfavorable - gusting winds, rain, sweltering heat, or sudden chills. Travel between Hexes takes either 2 Watches, or 1 Watch and each player in the Party takes one Fatigue.
  • On a 6, the weather is Terrible. Examples include thunderstorms, heavy rain, flash flooding or mudslides. Travel between Hexes takes 2 Watches and each Player in the Party takes one Fatigue.
  • If a 6 is rolled two days in a row, the weather becomes Catastrophic. Examples include Hurricanes, Blood Rain, Ash Clouds. Travel becomes practically impossible.

The dynamics of Time, Gear, and Skill apply to weather effects. Dedicated Gear relevant to the bad weather (e.g climbing equipment or rain hoods) or Skill relevant to the bad weather (e.g training in navigation or mountaineering) can mitigate the negative effects of bad weather.

Navigation and Investigation of Hexes

Each Hex is populated with difficult to navigate terrain, such as mountains, jungle, and woods. The Party takes one Watch to either Navigate to an adjacent Hex, or to Investigate the current Hex, uncovering a new location.

After performing these actions, an Encounter is then determined, which occurs at some point of the Party’s journey. Then the Party arrives at a location, and may spend the rest of a Watch exploring that location.

Navigation

To Navigate to an adjacent Hex, the Party chooses an adjacent Hex and, if known, a location within that Hex to travel to, then makes a Time, Gear & Skill Check (the Party has Time if they spend a Check Maps Token gained while Regrouping, and Skill if they have already discovered the location they are travelling to, though gaining these by other measures is also possible). If the Party Succeeds, they arrive at their planned end destination. If they Fail, roll a d6:

  • On a 1-3, the Party ends up back where they started.
  • On a 4, the Party veers left.
  • On a 5, the Party veers right.
  • On a 6, the Party arrives at their destination by sheer luck.

Veering into a Hex populated by ocean results in the Party finding themselves on a nondescript stretch of beach.

Investigation

To Investigate a Hex the Party currently occupies, the Party spends a Watch, then makes a Time, Gear, & Skill Check. On a Success, the Party discovers a new location within the Hex. On a Failure, use a d6 to determine a surrounding Hex that the party veers into. If possible, the Party travels to the adjacent Hex, potentially discovering a new location there instead.

Regrouping

Regrouping takes one Watch and allows each Character to individually choose from a variety of actions:

Check Maps

Gain a Check Maps Token, which lasts until the end of the next day, and may be used as a substitute for Time to prevent getting lost when navigating Hexes.

Forecast

Use magic or experience to forecast the weather. The GM rolls a d6: on a 1-2, they roll a random forecast on the weather table. On a 3-4, they roll tomorrow’s weather early, and relay this information. On a 5-6, they roll the day after next’s weather early, and relay this information.

Boost Morale

Share a riveting tale, sing a song, or otherwise inspire. Everyone recovers 1 WIL, or 2 WIL if the performer has any relevant skills or background.

Cook Food

Prepare food from gathered ingredients. Simple Meals require a fire, and Fancy Meals require a fire and Cooking Pot.

Gathering

There are several types of Gathering:

  • Hunting requires a bow or spear, and yields raw meat.
  • Foraging requires no tools and yields fruit and vegetables, and in addition, has a 1 in 6 chance of finding Wildcrafting materials.
  • Fishing requires a fishing rod or spear and yields raw fish.

Yield depends on the terrain (quantified in # of Portions of food). Character explicitly skilled in an activity may upgrade the die roll by one tier, i.e. from 1 to d4 to d6 to d8. Up to three portions of any one ingredient may occupy an inventory slot.

Wildcrafting

Wildcrafting allows one to craft healing salves, teas, and tonics, with ingredients found in the wild. Ingredients can be found while foraging and consist of mushrooms, mosses, plants, roots, bark, and saps. An ingredient’s rarity determines it’s Potency (1 for Common, 2 for Uncommon,and 4 for Rare). Furthermore, ingredients are defined by the type of region they are gathered from.

Each recipe has a Potency that must be met by combining ingredients. Each recipe must contain at least 2 different ingredients, and some recipes have requirements for region. Recipes may be learned from sages, and other wood-wise types.

Camping, Eating, and Sleeping

The Party may decide to set up camp for the day, choosing a number of Watches to camp for. Characters who camp for 2 Watches recover Fatigue. The Party may choose to take a free Regrouping Action, but this generates noise, and may bring undesirable attention to the Party’s camp.

Characters must sleep for at least 1 Watch, or become Deprived of sleep. Characters who only camp for 1 Watch are not Deprived, but do not recover Fatigue from sleep.

Characters must also must eat at least one portion of food a day to avoid becoming Deprived of food:

  • Desperate Meals are only eaten when in dire need of food, such as raw meat and fish, snakes, treefrogs, rats, and so on. WIL Save to not puke it up.
  • Fresh Meals consist of foraged fruit and vegetables that are otherwise not prepared.
  • Simple Meals are ingredients that have seen some basic level of preparation, such as grilled meat and roasted vegetables. Restores 1 Fatigue. Requires a fire.
  • Fancy Meals are made from multiple prepared ingredients put together, such as boiled vegetables with grilled fish, or boar meat sandwiches. This restores all Fatigue. Requires Fire and a Cooking Pot (Bulky).

Jungle

For each Jungle biome, roll on the Foliage Density. In Light Jungle or Mountainous Jungle roll d6, in Heavy Jungle roll 2d6. In Mountainous Jungle, also roll for Elevation.
